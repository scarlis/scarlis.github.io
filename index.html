Professional Background

Sam Carlisle provides a unique, multidisciplinary approach to emergency systems by blending psychological theory with over a decade of hands-on operational experience. This dual perspective allows for the creation of technical solutions that account for the human factor—specifically how the brain processes information during a crisis.

The academic foundations are rooted in a Bachelor of Science from Curtin University (Psychometrics and Research Methods) and a Diploma of Education focusing on High-Impact Instructional Methods.

Operational expertise includes 10+ years within the State Emergency Service (SES) and 14+ years in education leadership as a Deputy Principal. Technical certifications include AIIMS (DFES), emergency driving, bush firefighting, and land search techniques.
